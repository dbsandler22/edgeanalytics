How Edge Works

01

Athletes Train Between Sessions

Athletes complete short, expert-designed athletic challenges on their own — building strength, speed, agility, coordination, and endurance.

Each workout contributes to an athlete's Edge Score, a measurable indicator of athletic development.

02

Progress Becomes Visible

Athletes earn an Edge Score based on performance across the five dimensions of athletic development.

Coaches, families, and athletes see development trends and where improvement is needed. Athletes can compare their Edge Score and progress with teammates and peers of similar age and sport.

03

Trainers Earn Recurring Revenue

Athletes subscribe to access Edge training and challenges.

As athletes subscribe and participate, Edge shares a portion of each monthly subscription with their trainers — income that grows alongside their athletes.

Participation is free for trainers.

Why We Built Edge

Progress Shouldn't Be Invisible.

Youth sports are becoming more competitive and more specialized than ever.

What Research Shows

  • Early specialization is linked to higher rates of overuse injury
  • Repeating the same patterns limits diversity of movement development
  • Athletes often strengthen some capacities while neglecting others

Over time, those gaps in an athlete's foundation compound.

Great athletes develop through a balanced athletic foundation — speed, strength, agility, endurance, and coordination.

Edge makes athletic development visible, measurable, and adaptive.
