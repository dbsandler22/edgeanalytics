Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything you need to know about Edge Athletics and how it works for trainers.

How do I make money with Edge Athletics?

Athletes subscribe to Edge through the App Store, just like any other app.

When they sign up using your unique trainer code, a portion of their monthly subscription is automatically attributed to you.

We pay trainers monthly, after the first full month of athlete participation. The more athletes connected to you, the more recurring revenue you generate.

What does it cost me to join?

There is no cost for trainers to join Edge.

No subscription fee. No setup fee. No platform charge.

Athletes pay a monthly subscription, and you participate in that recurring revenue.

How much does the athlete subscription cost?

Athletes subscribe through the App Store for a monthly fee. Pricing is designed to be accessible for families while creating meaningful recurring income for trainers.

Exact pricing may evolve as we refine the platform.

How do I get started?

We've made it simple:

  1. You sign up as a trainer.
  2. You receive a unique trainer code.
  3. Your athletes download the app and enter your code when they register.

That's it. Once athletes are connected to you and begin subscribing, you start earning recurring revenue.

Why isn't sports-specific content available yet?

We intentionally designed Phase One to be simple.

Right now, Edge focuses on foundational athletic development — the physical qualities that underpin all sports.

Sports-specific modules and trainer-created content are part of the roadmap, but we're prioritizing ease of onboarding and immediate revenue for trainers first.

What exactly is Edge Athletics?

Edge Athletics is a trainer-first performance system that combines proven athletic foundation programming with measurable analytics and recurring revenue leverage.

It helps youth trainers extend their impact beyond in-session hours, show clear progress to parents, and build scalable income.

How is Edge connected to VSP?

Edge is powered by the philosophy and programming developed at VSP — a physical youth performance facility in Northport, NY where athletes train in semi-private, customized sessions.

The system inside Edge was refined in a real training environment before being built digitally.

What do athletes actually see inside the app?

Athletes receive structured performance programs focused on foundational athletic qualities like speed, strength, agility, mobility, and durability.

They can:

  • Track their progress over time
  • See performance benchmarks
  • Compare against relevant peer groups (age, gender, team, sport where appropriate)
  • Stay motivated through measurable improvement

The platform is designed to be motivating, not labeling. It encourages growth, consistency, and healthy development.

Why focus on foundational athletic development?

Early sports specialization has been linked to increased injury risk in youth athletes.

Edge emphasizes broad athletic development to build more durable, resilient athletes who can perform consistently and stay available for practices and games.

A stronger foundation benefits every sport.

Is this replacing my coaching?

No.

Edge complements your coaching. It provides structured foundational work between sessions and measurable tracking that strengthens your credibility.

You remain the coach. Edge gives you leverage.

Will this actually help with athlete retention?

Yes.

When athletes can see their progress — and parents can understand it — engagement increases. Transparency builds trust. Trust builds retention.

How do you ensure quality and consistency?

All foundational programming is built around a defined athletic philosophy developed through VSP's live training environment.

As the platform evolves, quality standards will remain central to maintaining credibility and long-term athlete development.

Is this only for local athletes in New York?

No.

VSP is the physical birthplace of the system in Northport, NY. Edge is built to scale nationally.

What's coming next?

Future phases include expanded sport-specific skill pathways and opportunities for trainers to contribute content within a structured quality framework.

We're building carefully to protect quality while expanding opportunity.
