FAQ

Common questions,
straight answers.

Select your perspective below. Questions are organized separately for families and trainers.

What is Edge Athletics and what does it do for my athlete?

Edge Athletics is a youth sports performance platform that gives athletes a quantified picture of their athletic development. Athletes log training sessions and performance benchmarks across six dimensions — speed, agility, strength, power, endurance, and recovery. The platform synthesizes this data into an Edge Score: a single number that represents overall athletic capability, with drill-down detail by dimension.

The goal is to make athletic development visible and measurable so athletes, parents, and coaches can see progress clearly over time — not just rely on gut feel or game performance.

How much does Edge Athletics cost for families?

The athlete subscription is a flat monthly fee. Exact pricing will be communicated to waitlist members before the launch. Families on the early waitlist will receive advance notice of pricing and may be eligible for early access rates.

Does my child need a trainer or coach to use Edge Athletics?

No. Athletes can join independently and log their own benchmarks without being connected to a trainer. However, connecting to a trainer through the platform unlocks additional features: trainer-assigned programming, coach visibility into your progress, and the ability to be referred for certified assessments.

If your athlete already works with a coach who is an Edge Athletics trainer partner, they will give you an invite code to link your athlete to their roster.

What are “certified benchmarks” and does my athlete need them?

Certified benchmarks are performance measurements taken during an in-person assessment at Victory Sports Performance in Northport, NY — observed, measured, and verified by a coach. They carry a certification badge in the app and apply a higher accuracy weighting to the Edge Score calculation.

Certified benchmarks are not required. Athletes who only self-report still get a valid Edge Score and full access to the platform. Certification is an optional enhancement for athletes who want verified data and are located near Long Island.

Is my athlete’s data private? Who can see it?

Athlete data is private by default. Only the athlete and their parent or guardian can see full profile details. If the athlete is connected to a trainer on the platform, that trainer can see their progress data — which is the point of the trainer-athlete relationship.

Leaderboards are opt-in. Athletes are never placed in public rankings without explicit consent. We do not sell athlete data to third parties. See our Trust & Safety page and Privacy Policy for full details.

What ages is Edge Athletics designed for?

Edge Athletics is designed for youth athletes, generally ages 8 through 18. The Edge Score calculation is normalized by age group so athletes are compared against relevant peers, not older or younger cohorts.

Parental consent and account management is required for athletes under 13 in compliance with COPPA guidelines.

Is this only for athletes on Long Island?

No. Edge Athletics is a digital platform accessible to athletes anywhere. The self-reporting and app-based features work nationwide.

The in-person certified assessment program is currently exclusive to Long Island (at Victory Sports Performance in Northport, NY). Certified assessment availability in other regions may expand in the future.

When will Edge Athletics be available? How do I get early access?

Edge Athletics is in its pre-launch phase. The best way to get early access is to join the family waitlist. Waitlist families will be notified first when the platform opens, will receive onboarding materials ahead of the general public, and may be eligible for early pricing.

What does Edge Athletics do for me as a trainer or coach?

Edge Athletics gives trainers visibility beyond their in-person hours. You get a dashboard showing every athlete on your roster: their Edge Score, dimension breakdowns, session completion, and benchmark progress. You can see who is training, who is improving, and where each athlete needs attention — without waiting for their next in-person session.

The platform also generates shareable progress reports you can use in parent conversations to demonstrate your coaching impact with objective data, not just anecdotes. And as long as your athletes stay active, you receive ongoing support compensation for implementing and sustaining the platform.

How does the compensation model work?

After an athlete’s first full month of active participation on the platform, you receive $1.99 per active athlete per month. This recurs as long as the athlete stays active — it is not a one-time bonus. Compensation accumulates automatically and is paid on a regular schedule (details provided at onboarding).

This compensation is structured to recognize your role in bringing athletes onto the platform and sustaining their engagement over time — not as a passive referral payment, but as support for your active implementation work.

Does this replace any part of my existing coaching or training business?

No. Edge Athletics is a data and visibility layer designed to extend your coaching, not substitute for it. The platform does not generate training plans, replace your methodology, or position itself as a coaching product. It tracks and quantifies athletic progress — the actual coaching decisions remain entirely yours.

The positioning to athletes and families is always that Edge Athletics exists to support their work with their trainer, not to replace it.

How do athletes join my roster on the platform?

You receive a unique trainer code during onboarding. When athletes register on the Edge Athletics platform, they enter your code to link to your dashboard. From that point forward, their progress appears in your roster view and you have coach visibility into their activity and scores.

You can share your code however you prefer: verbally, in a parent email, printed on a card, etc. The trainer toolkit includes a parent announcement email template for exactly this purpose.

Can I see which athletes are not staying active?

Yes. Your trainer dashboard shows active vs. inactive status for every athlete on your roster. You can see who has logged a session recently and who has gone quiet. This makes it easy to identify athletes who may need a re-engagement nudge — which also matters for your compensation, since the per-athlete payment only applies to active athletes.

What does “early partner” status mean?

Trainers who express interest now and are onboarded in the first cohort receive early partner status, which includes: priority setup before the general trainer pool, a dedicated onboarding walkthrough (not just a self-serve guide), access to all trainer toolkit materials before athletes arrive, and priority scheduling for athlete certified assessments at Victory Sports Performance.

Early partner status is about making your rollout smoother and faster than trainers who join later.

Do I need to be located on Long Island to be a trainer partner?

No. Trainers anywhere can join the platform and access the full roster dashboard, athlete progress tracking, and compensation model. The Long Island location is only relevant for the certified in-person assessment program at Victory Sports Performance. Remote or out-of-area trainers simply work with self-reported benchmarks for their athletes, which is the primary data source for the majority of users on the platform.

How do I get started?

Fill out the trainer interest form on the For Trainers page. Early partners receive priority setup and a dedicated onboarding walkthrough. We will contact you directly to confirm your spot and schedule your onboarding before the general launch.
